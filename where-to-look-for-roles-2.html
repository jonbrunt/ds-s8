Overview

This section is going to look the exact same every time you get to it because we want you to get comfortable and familiar with the content! Be sure to try out different job boards each time so you know the ones you like when you really dive into the job search. If you aren’t applying, that is OK. We just want you to see what’s out there and understand how to find the opportunities.

If you are looking, some part-time or contract work may be the best option so that you can do it amidst your already busy schedule. You can find these roles across many platforms but you’ll find some job boards specific to part-time/contract roles listed first.

Before you get started using any job board keep these tips in mind:

  • Be sure to set your filters to Date Posted = last 24 hours or last week. If the role has been posted for more than a week the company has most likely started interviewing. Spend your time focusing on roles that are still early in the process. 
  • Don’t reject yourself before you apply! Make sure that you meet at least 50% of the job requirements. This includes years of experience — aim for jobs that call for 0-3 years of relevant experience.
  • Use these boards as tools to source roles but apply for the role on the company website. Avoid the easy-apply portal!
  • Skip the cover letter, unless it is explicitly required. They are almost never read. Instead, send a cold (or warm) outreach message to help get your application seen. 
  • Ensure you complete every box on the application — even if it is just to insert N/A.
  • If asked about the desired salary, avoid giving a number. List market rate for {role} in {location} or negotiable. If you must insert a number, list 0. This does not mean you will work for no pay but indicates you are open to discussion.

Select a job board from the list provided above.

  1. Use the job board you selected to search for job titles that you are now eligible for based on the skills you’ve learned. 
  2. Set the filter to past 24 hours or past week
  3. Look through the job descriptions and find one you feel you meet 50% of the qualifications.
  4. If you would like to apply go ahead and submit your application!
